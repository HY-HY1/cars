MOT

Buying a car with no mot.

When browsing for a car on an auction house you want to check previous mots to ensure you are not going to have to complete a lot of work to pass it. 

EXAMPLE (find pictures and examples for guide)
Car with tyre advisories (£200-£400+ for a whole set of tyres)
Brake pads (£200-£500+) (CHECK PREVIOUS MOTS)
Lights, indicators, ETC ETC 

You can be stuck with heavy extra costs eating into your profit. This is especially important for older cheaper flips as you may buy a (example) corsa for £1000 selling for about £1500 on facebook. If it needs new tyres (200) new break pads (250) an mot (50) youll now likely make a loss on the car with transportation, insurance etc costs)
